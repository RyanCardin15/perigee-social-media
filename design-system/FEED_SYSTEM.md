# Perigee Feed System v2

Perigee's feed should feel like a premium coastal field guide: cinematic at
first glance, exact on inspection, and calm enough to trust. Every carousel is
made of five complete slide images generated by Codex built-in image generation
from point-for-point matched Perigee and NOAA data.

## Creative idea: Signal in the water

Generated water, typography, data, and information design form one coherent
editorial image. Water supplies emotion without depicting local conditions;
the exact prompt supplies every visible fact. No post slide is assembled from a
generated backdrop plus SVG, PNG, HTML, canvas, or deterministic overlays.

The five-slide story is fixed:

1. **Hook** — concise signal and the predicted high.
2. **Signal** — exact height, datum, station-local time, date, and station.
3. **Curve** — the matched seven-day NOAA/Perigee prediction series.
4. **Meaning** — Perigee's station-specific top-1% threshold or weekly range.
5. **Action** — prediction/observation boundary, source, disclaimer, and CTA.

## Visual language

- **Canvas:** 1080×1350 (4:5), with critical content inside 90-pixel side and
  100-pixel top/bottom safe areas.
- **Palette:** deep ocean ink `#071d2a`, warm foam `#faf9f4`, tide teal
  `#0099a8`, signal magenta `#c41e6a`, light magenta `#ff70ad`, and threshold
  brass `#a06a0a`.
- **Type:** elegant editorial serif for headlines and hero values; clean modern
  sans serif for facts, labels, and navigation.
- **Shape:** large rounded data surfaces, fine tidal contours, strong
  asymmetry, and generous negative space.
- **Contrast:** warm foam on deep ink for story surfaces and deep ink on warm
  foam for data surfaces. Accent color is semantic.

## Full-generation rules

Each `generation-brief.json` contains five independent prompts with an EXACT
TEXT block and the relevant frozen data. Codex generates each finished slide,
then visually compares it with the brief. Any misspelling, wrong character,
wrong decimal, missing point, invented label, clipping, or factual implication
requires regeneration.

The model may create text, numbers, charts, axes, and data cards because those
are the finished post. It must not create maps, recognizable station geography,
NOAA seals, warnings, flooding, observed-condition claims, or provider marks.
Post-generation processing is limited to image orientation, 1080×1350 sizing,
and JPEG encoding for Meta.

## Production contract

`social:prepare` freezes matched data and writes five prompts.
Codex generates and inspects all five complete images. `social:attach` records
the generator, prompt hash, source-image hash, dimensions, and checksum, then
performs format-only normalization and validation. A post cannot validate
without five reviewed Codex image outputs and no deterministic composition.
